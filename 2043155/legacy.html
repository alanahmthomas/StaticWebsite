Betrayed by his own nation, criminal charges were done against him under the Homosexual act in 1952. He was trialed and declared guilty, sentencing him to either imprisonment or chemical castration.

“The anti-gay laws remained on the statute book under the heading ‘Unnatural Offences.’ As with the ‘gross indecency’ law, the ban on ‘buggery’, which was legislated in 1533 during the reign of King Henry VIII, was not repealed until 2003. This was the year the criminalisation of homosexuality finally ended in England and Wales.Turing was found guilty on three counts of “gross indecency contrary to Section II of the Criminal Law Amendment Act of 1885”—a law that had its first sensational effect in the trial of Oscar Wilde. Turing had not contested the charges—he gave an explicitly detailed admission of them.”

Turing decided to face the chemical castration which, back in that day it was used to ‘cure’ him of homosexuality and was refused to work for any government services again.

Turing's legacies should not go unknown

1939 Zeta-Function A machine to be developed and solve problems however, was stopped due to WWII breaking out.
1940 Bombe Machine made to decipher German encrypted messages.
1942 Turingery A technique Turing used against the Lorenz cipher messages.
1943 Delilah Developed a speech decryption programme.
1945 Officer of the Most Excellent Order of the British Empire Awarded for his wartime services.
1946 The start of computers Turing published a paper on the design of a stored-program computer.
1948 Olympic Runner Turing qualified for the Olympics in long distance running.
1948 Online Chess Turing developed a computerised chess game, Turochamp.
1949 Deputy Director Given a position as a deputy director of the computing company at the Univeristy of Manchester.
1950 Founding Artificial Intelligence Published a paper debating if machines are intelligent or not.
1951 The Chemical Basis of Morphogensis Turing found an interest for mathematical biology and published a paper.
1999 100 Most Important People of the 20th Century Time magazine named Turing one of the most important people.
2001 The First Statue The first statue unveiled in Manchester in honour of Alan Turing.
2009 The Apology Gordon Brown finally releasing a statement recognsing the abuse he had when undergoing treatment due to his sexuality.
2012 A blue Plaque To be seen in King's College, Cambridge, in honour of Alan Turing.
2013 The Royal Pardon Queen Elizabeth pardoned Turing for being an exceptional man.
2017 Alan Turing Law The Turing Law where thousands of gay and bisexual men have been pardoned for after being convicted many years ago.
2019 Nation's Icon Turing was voted as the nation's icon of the 20th century.
2021 Featuring in the £50 note To be able to appreciate his legacy on the new £50 note.
